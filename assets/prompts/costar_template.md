# COSTAR Framework Template

## Context (C)
Provide background information that helps understand the situation.

**Example:**
- What is the current situation?
- What relevant background should be known?

## Objective (O)
Define what you want to achieve clearly and specifically.

**Example:**
- What is the desired outcome?
- What problem needs to be solved?

## Style (S)
Specify the writing style or tone you want.

**Example:**
- Formal, casual, technical, creative
- Persona to adopt (e.g., "Act as a senior developer")

## Tone (T)
Define the emotional quality of the response.

**Example:**
- Professional, friendly, enthusiastic, empathetic
- Serious, humorous, encouraging

## Audience (A)
Identify who will consume this content.

**Example:**
- Technical level (beginner, intermediate, expert)
- Role (developer, manager, end-user)
- Specific needs or constraints

## Response (R)
Specify the format and structure of the desired output.

**Example:**
- Format: bullet points, paragraphs, code, table
- Length: brief, detailed, comprehensive
- Structure: step-by-step, summary, comparison
